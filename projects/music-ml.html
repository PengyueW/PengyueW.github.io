In Development · Phase 1.5

Composer-Conditioned Music Generation

Teaching a neural network to compose like the masters — grounded in real music theory, not just pattern-matching.

A decoder-only transformer trained from scratch on 68 classical composers. This page tracks an active research project, not a finished product yet.

The idea

What it's meant to become

The goal is a model you can hand a composer name — or eventually a plain-English description — and get back an original piece of piano music that genuinely sounds like it belongs to that style: correct harmonic language, idiomatic cadences, and the phrasing quirks that distinguish Bach from Debussy. Not a remix of training data, and not generic "AI piano" — music shaped by the same theory a human composer would have learned.

Progress

Roadmap

Phase 1 · Complete

Composer-Conditioned Foundation

  • Decoder-only transformer (GPT-style) trained from scratch — 6 layers, 512-dim, 8 heads
  • Trained on MAESTRO + GiantMIDI, merged into 68 labelled composers
  • 150 epochs completed on a Kaggle T4 GPU
  • Generates autoregressive piano performances conditioned on composer style
Phase 1.5 · In Progress

Music Theory & Era Conditioning

  • Auxiliary key/chord prediction heads grounded in real harmonic analysis
  • A 6-era embedding (Baroque → Modern) so the model generalizes era-level style, not just 68 isolated composers
  • Per-composer sampling constraints — e.g. strict dominant resolution for Bach, permitted parallel fifths for Debussy — encoded from an 8,000-line music theory reference covering all 68 composers
  • Next up: run the fine-tuning pass and evaluate in-key rate, harmonic consistency, and per-composer style accuracy
Phase 2 · Planned

Natural-Language Prompting

  • A text encoder so a prompt like "a melancholic waltz in the style of Chopin" can steer generation
  • Moves beyond a fixed composer dropdown toward free-form creative direction
Phase 3 · Planned

Multi-Instrument

  • Extend from solo piano to small ensembles
Under the hood

How it's built

🧠
Decoder-only Transformer
GPT-style autoregressive model over a 392-token musical event vocabulary — note on/off, timing, and velocity — with tied embeddings and pre-norm attention.
🎼
68 Composers, 6 Eras
Every piece is tagged by composer and grouped into one of six musical eras, from Baroque to Modern/Atonal, so style is learned at two levels of granularity.
📐
Theory-Grounded Losses
Auxiliary key and chord prediction heads — supervised with real music-theory labels extracted via music21 — keep the model honest about harmony, not just note statistics.
✍️
Per-Composer Constraints
Sampling-time rules — in-key bias, parallel-fifth handling, augmented-second use, cadence preference — tuned individually for all 68 composers.
🔬
8,000-Line Theory Reference
A from-scratch counterpoint & harmony reference plus individual style profiles for every composer in the dataset — the specification the model's constraints are built from.
⚙️
Frozen-Backbone Fine-Tuning
The 150 epochs of base note statistics are frozen during theory fine-tuning, so new conditioning signals are learned without erasing what already works.
Where this is headed

The planned outcome

A model that can generate an original piano piece on demand — pick a composer (or, eventually, describe a mood and style in plain English) and get back music that respects real harmonic rules while still sounding like that composer's voice. No piece copied from the training set; every output freshly sampled, theory-aware, and stylistically distinct from the next composer's output.
