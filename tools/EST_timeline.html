Start of CHESS mission

EPFL students and the EPFL Space Center came together to laydown the basis of what would be the CHESS mission. From the start, the goal was very ambitious: to go beyond theory and build real spacecraft systems, to design and launch CubeSats from scratch, not just participate in competitions. The CHESS mission—was initiated at EPFL’s space ecosystem: Initially conceived as a constellation of small satellites, it aimes at collecting scientific measurements (e.g., astrophysics / atmosphere) and tech demonstrations.

September 2019
×

Foundation of EST

EPFL Spacecraft team is officially created and registered as a MAKE project and an EPFL association. The team started organizing itself into engineering sub-teams including software, electronics, structures, and telecom, and they worked on designing a CubeSat platform; developing subsystems like onboard computer (OBC), communications, and power systems; launching student-oriented projects like CanSat (mini satellites for training) and early prototypes for subsystems. Importantly, their mission evolved from pure research toward hands-on engineering + industry collaboration.

January 2023
×

Bunny launch mission

Bunny, a prototype of the onboard computer, was the first proper mission by EST: On January 31, the EPFL Spacecraft Team's onboard computer Bunny was launched in California, USA, hosted on a D-Orbit spacecraft as part of Starlink's 2-6 mission. This is the first time anything made at EPFL has been launched into space since 2009.

January 2025
×

Twocan computer

Twocan is our last onboard computer that was flown in January 2025 and is still orbiting Earth right now. Learning from the flaws of Bunny, Twocan aims to provide a more powerful and redundant onboard computer. This redundancy ensures that in the event of a computer failure, the other can take over.

April 2026
×

Critical design review and testing stage

After completing the design phase and the critical design review in April 2026, EST is ready to move on to the next phase in the journey, which is the testing and building stage of the satellite. The team has a lot to do within this 1 year window before launch, including finalising the prototype and constructing fully functional systems that are well-supported and tested to the maximum.

Launching of our CHESS satellite into space!

The final version of the satellite is assembled and placed onto the carrier rocket, ready for its grand entry launch into the Exosphere.

Future?

The CHESS journey will continue with Pathfinder 1 and Pathfinder 2, to form a constellation of scientific satellites orbiting Earth in different orbits. This is an ambitious project that will span over half a decade, but Pathfinder 0 will lay solid foundations for the days to come.
